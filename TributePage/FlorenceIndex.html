Florence Nightingale

The life and times of 'The Lady with the Lamp'

Timeline of Florence's Life:

  • 1820: She was born in Tuscany, Italy. At age 16, Florence, who always had a very keen interest in philanthropy and caring for the sick, knew her calling was nursing.
  • 1843: Decides to work in hospitals
  • 1844: A career in nursing was looked down upon by society. After much opposition, Florence announced her decision to enter the nursing field in 1844.
  • 1847: Visits hospitals in Italy and observes Catholic sisters fulfilling nursing duties
  • 1851 - 1853: Florence returns to Kaiserswerth for nursing training. She then became Superintendent of the Establishment for Gentlewomen in 1853.
  • 1853: She enrolled as a student at the Lutheran Hospital of Pastor Fliedner in Kaiserwerth, Germany.
  • 1853: The Crimean War broke out. Nightingale received a letter from Secretary of War, Sidney Herbett - both eventually became very good friends - requesting assistance from her nurses to tend to the soldiers. She assembled a team of more than 30 nurses and sailed to Crimea immediately. Nightingale quickly got to work and tried to lower the death rate.
  • 1854 March: Britain, France and Turkey declare war on Russia. Florence recruited to oversee the introduction of female nurses into the military hospitals in Turkey.
  • 1854 November: Florence arrives in Scutari with 38 nurses. Mary SeacoleMary Seacole arrived in London and offers her services to Mrs Elizabeth Herbert who was in charge of recruiting a second group of nurses to join Florence in Scutari, but is rejected.
  • 1854: Crimean War begins
  • 1855: Contracts Crimean Fever and almost dies
  • 1855: Mary Seacole funds her own passage to the Crimea. She also purchases medicines and home comforts that would be useful for the military. Florence and Mary meet and see much of each other in Balaclava.
  • 1855: The Nightingale fund was set up to open up a training school for nurses. By 1860, 50,000 had been collected and The Nightingale School and Home for Nurses was established at St. Thomas Hospital. She closely watched the progress of the institution.
  • 1856: Crimean War ends; returns to England; declines wedding proposal of Sir Harry Verney
  • 1856 March: The war was over. An estimated 94000 men were sent to the war front, out of which almost 4000 died of battle wounds, 19000 died of diseases and 13000 were invalidated out of the Army.
  • 1856 August: Florence returned from the Crimean War. Florence returned to England as a national hero but she was deeply shocked by the mass death that took place. Therefore, she was determined to begin a campaign that would improve the quality of nursing in military hospitals. She started investigating before the Royal Commission on the Health of the Army and that resulted in the formation of the Army Medical College.
  • 1860: Florence became the first woman to be elected a fellow of the Statistical Society. Florence established the Nightingale Training School for Nurses. In total Florence published 200 books, reports and pamphlets during her lifetime.
  • 1861: Assists U.S. in organizing soldiers' hospitals in Civil War
  • 1897: Diamond Jubilee of Queen Victoria includes exhibition of Florence Nightingale's nursing contributions
  • 1883: In recognition of Florence's hard work, she received the Royal Red Cross from Queen Victoria.
  • 1907: Florence receives the Order of Merit - becoming the first woman to receive it.
  • 1910: Florence dies at her home in the West End of London and was buried at St Margaret's East Wellow.
